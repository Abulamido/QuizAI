The Problem

  • Manual Grading Overhead: Educators spend disproportionate amounts of time marking open-ended and essay questions.
  • Delayed Feedback: Students often wait weeks to understand their mistakes, hindering the learning process.
  • Inconsistent Evaluation: Human fatigue causes bias and inconsistencies when grading large classes.
  • Scalability Limitations: Traditional manual grading fails to scale effectively in modern online paradigms.

The Solution: QuizAI

An intelligent, cloud-based platform that automates grading and feedback.

  • Instant Turnaround: Generative AI evaluates thousands of submissions in seconds.
  • Analytical Feedback: Students receive detailed explanations of *why* they lost points, not just a raw score.
  • Educator Empowerment: Lecturers can define detailed grading rubrics for the AI to follow strictly.
  • Modern Interface: High-performance Next.js frontend with secure role-based access.

System Architecture

Core Technology Stack

Next.js 14+ (App Router) React & TypeScript Supabase (PostgreSQL) Google Gemini AI Vanilla CSS (Glassmorphism)

The system employs a serverless architecture where Supabase handles authentication and persistent data storage logic. Next.js handles server-side rendering, and the Google Gemini API acts as our intelligent middleware for interpreting student answers against grading criteria.

How the AI Grades

  • Context Injection: The system sends the original question, full rubric, and the student's text to the LLM backend.
  • Semantic Analysis: Gemini parses the intent and factual accuracy rather than just looking for strict keywords.
  • Score Generation: Output is parsed into structured JSON containing a numerical score and qualitative feedback strings.

Conclusion

QuizAI successfully demonstrates that Large Language Models can augment the educational sector securely and intuitively. It shifts the paradigm from rote assessment to immediate, personalized educational feedback.
